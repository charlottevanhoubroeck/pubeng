CHARLOTTE VANHOUBROECK 
ILLUSTRATION


I always carry a sketchbook with me. Ready to be drawn when a witty moment frolics by. Those moments contain story seeds. They keep the eyes focused, the imagination fuelled, the limits non-existent. And most of all, they keep the fun alive.

Bigger stories are told through woodcuts. I carve them in birch wood and print them in black. As if the warm grained surface of the wood provokes me to uncover images that hide beneath. Images that invite the viewer into peculiar architectural environments in which all sorts of characters tend to find the most unhoped-for struggles. The splinters in my fingers and the wood shavings on my studio floor are proof of my enthusiasm for the woodcut.



The history of the arts provides me with endless inspiration. She offers me enriching tales and knowledge that form a steady framework to build upon. For example, there are the graphics of artists such as Félix Vallotton, Edvard Munch, Käthe Kollwitz and Frans Masereel, which surprise me incessantly, tickle my imagination unceasingly and guide me very quietly when I carve my tales out of the wood.

Tales occurring in all shapes and sizes. Sometimes a nonsensical and absurd anecdote told by a drunk friend, other times a grand story once written by a melancholic virtuoso. Tales that astonish, freshen the mind, help to reinvent the everyday world. Tales that make me see how the alliance between spoken words and visual images is one of the greatest pleasures for an illustrator to enjoy.

And now go have a look at my work. It’s fun, I promise!
