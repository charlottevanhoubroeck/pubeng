CHRANDESYX - 'THE PILLARS'

Woodcuts, created for cd-coverdesign and posterdesign

 "After a thousand years of cruel wars between the cities on the mountains, Chrandesyx came out triumphant. On their way home from the battlefields, soldiers sang songs. However dreadful the wars and politics of Chrandesyx were, the people were united in their love for music. ..." 

Discover and listen to the three man band Chrandesyx. This year they will release their first and long awaited album 'The Pillars'. An album that needed some triumphant artwork.

" ... The great city of Chrandesyx dominated the land, from the swamps in the north to the white beaches in the south. Alas, everything changed when a new prince was born. Ferre the First of Chrandesyx was born mute, and as he came to power, he ordered that every citizen should have their tongue cut out. He could not bare to hear their singing, as he couldn't ever do it himself. Chrandesyx became 'The Quiet City'. There was no more music to be sung.

When the decree was executed, some people fled to the caves underneath the city. Their children grew up to become the musicians of the underground. But in the silent city, these boys and girls had to pretend to be mute. However, when the evening came, they would sing songs of aspirations and hope in the bars of Cobble Road. And when they played, they touched the hearts of the muted community. "

- text by Simon Debbaut (drums Chrandesyx)
